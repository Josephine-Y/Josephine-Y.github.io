ESC 101 Praxis I Cracking Eggs

Who: Team of 5

What: Develop a design concept for a problem in the EngSci community

When: Sep 2024 to Dec 2024

Where: University of Toronto

Why: ESC 101

In the first semester of first-year Engineering Science, I worked in a team of five for an Engineering Design course, ESC 101 Praxis I. The main project was to identify a Splartz—a minor inconvenience—within the first-year Engineering Science community and recommend a design for a solution. My team worked on addressing the opportunity of stray eggshell fragments contaminating egg contents after cracking a raw egg for consumption. This Splartz resonated with us as we had all experienced the frustration associated with removing tiny eggshell fragments when preparing eggs. At the end of the term, we produced a design report that compared our final design to alternatives, supporting our recommendation with evidence from testing and research.
