CIV 102 Bridge Design Project

Who: Team of 3

What: Construct a Matboard bridge

When: Nov 2024

Where: University of Toronto

Why: CIV 102

In the first semester of first-year Engineering Science, I worked in a team of three for a Civil Engineering course, CIV 102 Structures and Materials. Our objective was to apply the knowledge we had gained in the course to design and construct a bridge using a 32" x 40" x 0.05" sheet of Matboard and two tubes of concrete cement. My team iterated through multiple designs by analyzing the data gained from digitally calculated stress on various areas of the bridge under bending. We then constructed the bridge, also requiring optimization in the form of cutting the board such that all required pieces were obtained from the rectangular sheet we were provided. To conclude the project, we tested the load that our bridge—Trussworthy—could withstand using the teaching team's apparatus.
